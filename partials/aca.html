What is the Affordable Care Act

The Affordable Care Act puts consumers back in charge of their health care. Workforce Connections, Inc., through a subcontract from NWCEP and a grant from the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services, provides consumers and businesses with assistance in securing and understanding their health care options. Workforce Connections, Inc. staff conduct public educational workshops and one-on-one enrollment assistance.

Open enrollment begins on November 1, 2016 and ends on January 31, 2017. Some exceptions may apply. For more information call 800-742-5627 or 608-790-8136, or complete the Request Services portion of our website.

2017 Coverage

You may qualify for a special enrollment period for the Affordable Care Act ("Obamacare") and be enrolled for 2017 if:

  • You lose your job related health insurance
  • You have a child, adopt a child, get married or divorced
  • You moved to Wisconsin from another State
  • You are now on COBRA and want to drop it because it's too expensive
  • There were errors on your original enrollment through the Marketplace
  • You received misinformation regarding the Affordable Care Act
  • You were incarcerated but are not incarcerated now
  • You recently became a legal immigrant
  • You are no longer eligible for Wisconsin BadgerCare
  • You are aging out of Foster Care
  • You are reaching age of 26 and will no longer be covered by your Parents insurance
  • You applied in a timely manner through BadgerCare but were rejected and your application was not sent to the Marketplace in time to enroll there
  • There were exceptional circumstances in your life like a natural disaster

Other special enrollment information:

If you are a member of a federally recognized tribe you can apply any time during the year through the Health Insurance Marketplace. This does not make you ineligible for Indian Health Services (IHS).
