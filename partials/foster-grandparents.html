Foster Grandparents

What is Foster Grandparent (FGP)

The Foster Grandparent Program places adults 55 and older in schools to serve as role models, tutors, and friends to children with exceptional needs who would benefit from the one-on-one attention a Foster Grandparent can offer. The program provides a way for volunteers age 55 and over to stay active by tutoring children in reading and math. These efforts produce higher academic performance, instill confidence, and boost self-esteem that results in improved class participation and school attendance.

Additional Information

FGP is available in participating elementary schools in La Crosse, Buffalo, Jackson, Pepin and Juneau counties. Volunteers meeting eligibility requirements qualify for a stipend, meals, transportation costs, and more. Click on the Volunteer benefits link below for complete details. For more information on how to become a Foster Grandparent or a host school call 608-317-9133, or complete the Request Services portion of our website.
